President Donald J. Trump Wants to Run for a Third Term — Here's What You Need to Know
By Gabriel Landry | April 31st, 2025

When interviewed by Gabriel Landry with IN-CREDIBLE NEWS, D.J.T was questioned about his ambitions for a third term.

Interviewer: Mr. Trump, there's been speculation about your future in politics. Can you comment on the rumors you're considering a third run—this time, surprisingly, under the Democratic Party banner?

Donald Trump: "Well, a lot of people—a lot of very smart people—are saying it's a brilliant idea. Look, I've always been about results. The Republican leadership, frankly, they've lost their edge. Total disaster. So I said, why not? The Democrats—if they want to win, really win—they need a winner. And everybody knows I'm a winner. I'll do what Sleepy Joe didn't. They're begging me, honestly. I've always said I'm for the American people, not for one party."

Trump, 78, floated legal theories about how he might be able to pursue a third term, despite the 22nd Amendment to the U.S. Constitution, which prohibits anyone from being elected to the presidency more than twice.

"Well, look, the 22nd Amendment says two elected terms. That's what it says. But there are scholars — top scholars — who are saying there could be flexibility," Trump said, without offering specific names. "You could see Congress move on it. Maybe a joint resolution, maybe something under the Congressional Review Act, nobody's really looked at that angle before. Very interesting stuff."

When asked whether his flirtation with the Democratic Party was serious, Trump grinned.

"If they want to win — and I mean really win — they know who to call. I've done it before. Bigger than anyone. Could do it again. And let's be honest — the party labels, they're just labels. I care about results for real Americans."

A spokesperson for the Democratic National Committee did not respond to a request for comment.

"So yeah—I might run again. As a Democrat. Stranger things have happened, folks. And it would be tremendous, believe me."
